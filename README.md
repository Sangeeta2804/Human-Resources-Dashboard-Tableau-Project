# Human-Resources-Dashboard-Tableau-Project
Interactive HR Dashboard built using Tableau to analyze employee demographics, hiring trends, workforce distribution, performance ratings, and salary insights using interactive visualizations and filters.

## Project Overview

This project is an interactive Human Resources Dashboard developed in Tableau to analyze workforce trends, employee demographics, hiring patterns, termination rates, salary distribution, and departmental performance.

The dashboard helps HR teams and management make data-driven decisions regarding workforce planning, employee retention, and organizational performance.

### Business Objective

The primary goal of this dashboard is to:

Monitor employee hiring and termination trends
Analyze workforce demographics
Understand salary patterns across age groups and education levels
Identify departmental employee distribution
Evaluate employee performance
Support HR decision-making using visual insights
Dataset Information

The dataset contains HR-related information including:

Employee ID
Gender
Age
Department
Education level
Salary
Performance rating
Location
Hire date
Employee status
Termination details

## Dashboard Insights
### 1. Workforce Status Analysis
Active employees represent the majority of the workforce.
Total terminated employees are significantly lower than hired employees.
The company is showing overall workforce growth.

#### Insight:

The organization appears to be expanding with a relatively stable retention rate.

### 2. Department Distribution Analysis

Largest departments:

Operations → 2,429 employees
Sales → 1,634 employees
Customer Service → 1,489 employees
IT → 1,243 employees

Smallest departments:

HR
Finance
Marketing

#### Insight:

Operations and Sales require the largest workforce because of business demand.

### 3. Location Analysis

Employee distribution:

HQ → 70%
Branch Offices → 30%

#### Insight:

Most employees work at headquarters, indicating centralized operations.

### 4. Gender Analysis

Gender distribution:

Male → 54%
Female → 46%

#### Insight:

The workforce shows relatively balanced gender representation.

### 5. Education and Age Analysis

Most employees belong to:

25–34 age group
Bachelor's degree category

#### Insight:

The company mainly hires young professionals with undergraduate qualifications.

### 6. Education vs Performance Analysis

Observations:

Employees with Bachelor's degrees show strong performance ratings
Majority fall under:
Excellent
Good

#### Insight:

Higher educational qualifications appear associated with better performance.

### 7. Salary Analysis

Age vs Salary trend indicates:

Salary generally increases with age and experience
Mid-career professionals receive higher compensation

#### Insight:

Experience level positively impacts salary structure.
